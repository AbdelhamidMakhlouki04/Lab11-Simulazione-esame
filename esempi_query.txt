Archi diretti (JOIN tra due tabelle)
1. Cliente → Fattura — trova i clienti e le loro fatture (arco Customer → Invoice tramite CustomerId)
sqlSELECT c.CustomerId, c.FirstName || ' ' || c.LastName AS Cliente,
       i.InvoiceId, i.Total
FROM Customer c
JOIN Invoice i ON c.CustomerId = i.CustomerId
ORDER BY c.CustomerId;

2. Fattura → Riga di fattura — esplora l'arco Invoice → InvoiceLine, utile per capire le relazioni 1:N
sqlSELECT i.InvoiceId, i.InvoiceDate,
       COUNT(il.InvoiceLineId) AS NumeroRighe,
       SUM(il.UnitPrice * il.Quantity) AS TotaleRighe
FROM Invoice i
JOIN InvoiceLine il ON i.InvoiceId = il.InvoiceId
GROUP BY i.InvoiceId
ORDER BY NumeroRighe DESC;

3. Track → Album → Artist — percorso lungo 2 archi nella catena musicale
sqlSELECT t.TrackId, t.Name AS Brano,
       al.Title AS Album,
       ar.Name AS Artista
FROM Track t
JOIN Album al ON t.AlbumId = al.AlbumId
JOIN Artist ar ON al.ArtistId = ar.ArtistId
ORDER BY ar.Name, al.Title;

Archi con aggregazione
4. Quante tracce per genere? — arco Track → Genre, con conteggio
sqlSELECT g.Name AS Genere,
       COUNT(t.TrackId) AS NumeroBrani
FROM Genre g
LEFT JOIN Track t ON g.GenreId = t.GenreId
GROUP BY g.GenreId
ORDER BY NumeroBrani DESC;

5. Artisti senza album — archi mancanti (nodi isolati nel grafo)
sqlSELECT ar.ArtistId, ar.Name AS Artista
FROM Artist ar
LEFT JOIN Album al ON ar.ArtistId = al.ArtistId
WHERE al.AlbumId IS NULL;

6. Tracce in più playlist — l'arco PlaylistTrack è una relazione N:N tra Playlist e Track
sqlSELECT t.Name AS Brano,
       COUNT(pt.PlaylistId) AS NumeroPlaylist
FROM Track t
JOIN PlaylistTrack pt ON t.TrackId = pt.TrackId
GROUP BY t.TrackId
HAVING COUNT(pt.PlaylistId) > 1
ORDER BY NumeroPlaylist DESC;

Archi transitivi (catene di 3+ nodi)
7. Cliente → Fattura → Brano acquistato — percorso completo dal cliente al brano
sqlSELECT c.FirstName || ' ' || c.LastName AS Cliente,
       t.Name AS Brano,
       il.UnitPrice,
       il.Quantity
FROM Customer c
JOIN Invoice i    ON c.CustomerId = i.CustomerId
JOIN InvoiceLine il ON i.InvoiceId = il.InvoiceId
JOIN Track t      ON il.TrackId = t.TrackId
ORDER BY Cliente, Brano;

8. Dipendente → Cliente → Fattura — l'arco Employee → Customer (SupportRepId) concatenato fino alle fatture
sqlSELECT e.FirstName || ' ' || e.LastName AS Agente,
       COUNT(DISTINCT c.CustomerId)   AS NumClienti,
       COUNT(i.InvoiceId)             AS NumFatture,
       ROUND(SUM(i.Total), 2)         AS TotaleVendite
FROM Employee e
LEFT JOIN Customer c ON e.EmployeeId = c.SupportRepId
LEFT JOIN Invoice i  ON c.CustomerId = i.CustomerId
GROUP BY e.EmployeeId
ORDER BY TotaleVendite DESC;

Self-join (arco riflessivo)
9. Employee → Employee (ReportsTo) — l'unico arco che collega una tabella a se stessa
sqlSELECT e.FirstName || ' ' || e.LastName AS Dipendente,
       m.FirstName || ' ' || m.LastName AS Responsabile
FROM Employee e
LEFT JOIN Employee m ON e.ReportsTo = m.EmployeeId
ORDER BY Responsabile, Dipendente;

10. Gerarchia completa dei dipendenti — percorso ricorsivo sul self-join (SQLite/PostgreSQL con CTE ricorsiva)
sqlWITH RECURSIVE Gerarchia AS (
    SELECT EmployeeId,
           FirstName || ' ' || LastName AS Nome,
           ReportsTo,
           0 AS Livello
    FROM Employee
    WHERE ReportsTo IS NULL   -- radice della gerarchia

    UNION ALL

    SELECT e.EmployeeId,
           e.FirstName || ' ' || e.LastName,
           e.ReportsTo,
           g.Livello + 1
    FROM Employee e
    JOIN Gerarchia g ON e.ReportsTo = g.EmployeeId
)
SELECT REPEAT('  ', Livello) || Nome AS Struttura, Livello
FROM Gerarchia
ORDER BY Livello, Nome;

Archi "inversi" e nodi con alto grado
11. Artisti con più album — grado uscente del nodo Artist nel grafo Artist → Album
sqlSELECT ar.Name AS Artista,
       COUNT(al.AlbumId) AS NumeroAlbum
FROM Artist ar
JOIN Album al ON ar.ArtistId = al.ArtistId
GROUP BY ar.ArtistId
ORDER BY NumeroAlbum DESC
LIMIT 10;

12. Brano più acquistato — nodo Track con il maggior numero di archi entranti da InvoiceLine
sqlSELECT t.Name AS Brano,
       ar.Name AS Artista,
       COUNT(il.InvoiceLineId) AS Acquisti
FROM Track t
JOIN InvoiceLine il ON t.TrackId = il.TrackId
JOIN Album al ON t.AlbumId = al.AlbumId
JOIN Artist ar ON al.ArtistId = ar.ArtistId
GROUP BY t.TrackId
ORDER BY Acquisti DESC
LIMIT 10;
